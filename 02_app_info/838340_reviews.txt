I still decided to give it a thumbs up because of the touching narrative and the nice artwork. The writing, however, just has too many issues and is obviously in need of both proofreading and editing.\n\n[b]Yun[/b], our protagonist, is deeply concerned about his best friend [b]Max[/b], which got badly injured in an accident and lies in a coma ever since. Yun visits him every day, not only out of concern, or because he is romantically attracted to him, but also because he's able to talk and to interact with his friend since Yun has the ability to enter Max's dreams. The only thing Yun needs to do is to fall asleep near him.\n\nThere is no obvious clarification as to why he is able to visit the dreams of the comatose Max, but one route explains the circumstances for that, at least, vaguely. [spoiler]The book Yun reads describes that people who are really close to each other are able to enter the other person's dreams, as long as the feelings are strong enough. Every outcome leaves a bitter taste, there isn't a single ending that makes up for it. You should be aware of this before you give this Visual Novel a try. I do know that there are people that simply can't handle bad endings well if there is no happy end in sight that compensates the reader for suffering. The story ultimately revolves around loss, sorrow and how to deal with the feeling that you might have only little time left to spend with someone very dear to you. Honestly, this is the kind of story that usually makes me sob like a little girl, but this time I just felt nothing. No tears were shed, and the story touched me only a tiny bit. I'm pretty sure that the cause for that is the bad writing that just kind of failed to get me in the mood to actually care about the characters.\n\nhttps://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=1593938302\n\nThe English translation, or rather the localization, was handled pretty badly. It just makes it hard for me to take the story seriously, and since the story provided here is more heart-wrenching and serious than anything else, the bad localization regrettably ruined every tad of atmosphere for me. Except for the bad translation itself, it is also very confusing when the character names change rapidly between fore and last name, or when they address the protagonist simply as [i]the guy[/i]. [b][i]May's[/i][/b] story could be so touching if it wouldn't be for this single, yet deal-breaking issue. I'm very sorry that I mention it so often but it's the sole reason why I couldn't immerse myself into the story, which makes me terribly sad since the storyline alone is absolutely great.\n\nThe transition of all seasons in the main menu is really pretty, even if you might don't see much of that since you will most likely finish this game in one sitting, taken how short it is. The artwork is surprisingly nice as well, especially if you consider that this title only costs $3.99.
